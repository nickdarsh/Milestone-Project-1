The Evolution of the Invisible Handshake: The History of HTTP

Brief Abstract

Hypertext Transfer Protocol, better known as HTTP, is the invisible handshake that connects the universe of the World Wide Web. Starting its history as a simple protocol for sharing documents, the history of HTTP shows innovation, collaboration, and adaptation. It was created by a person who shaped the history of computing known as Sir Tim Berners Lee.

This website explores the pivotal moments in HTTP's development, from its initial, rough simplicity to the complex, efficient protocol that powers the modern internet.

Key Terms

  • HTTP: Hypertext Transfer Protocol
  • WWW: World Wide Web
  • GET Method: The original and most basic request method in HTTP 0.9.
  • Persistent Connections: Introduced in HTTP 1.1 to reuse a single TCP connection for multiple requests.
  • Multiplexing: Introduced in HTTP/2 to allow multiple requests and responses to be sent in parallel.
